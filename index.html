01 / The problem

Voice memos are where ideas go to die

There are two ways people sketch music without an instrument. They describe it — “dark trap beat, 140 BPM, heavy 808s” — or they perform it, beatboxing and humming straight into a voice memo. Text-to-music tools serve the describers well. But text captures style, not groove: not the pattern, the syncopation, the micro-timing, the accents that make a beat feel like something.

The performers? They record the memo, and the memo dies there. Turning it into an actual track means a DAW, instrument skills, production knowledge and hours of work. That distance between idea and listenable artifact is the single biggest drop-off in casual music creation.

Nobody serves the performer with a phone. That's the product.

True story

Charlie Puth beatboxed the bones of “Attention” into his iPhone's Voice Memos on a train in Tokyo, then produced it later in his hotel. The entire Voicenotes album started as voice memos.

He had a portable studio and a decade of production chops waiting at the hotel. Most people just have the memo.

02 / The golden path

App open to banger in six moves

  1. 01

    Record

    One big red button. Beatbox 4–8 bars — kick is boots, snare is cats, hi-hat is ts. Or just make sounds; we'll figure it out. No metronome needed: tempo is inferred from your take, within ±2 BPM.

  2. 02

    Hear it back — instantly

    Under three seconds after you stop, your beat plays back on a real kit, looped, with a scrolling grid of exactly what the app heard. All on-device. No signal, no problem.

  3. 03

    Fix anything in two taps

    Missed hit? Tap the cell. Snare heard as kick? Long-press to relabel. Drag the quantize slider from Loose to Tight — micro-timing is stored as a groove template, so tightening the grid never flattens your feel.

  4. 04

    Hum a melody optional

    A second pass over the looping beat. Pitch-tracked to MIDI, key inferred, scale-snap if you want it — rendered as a bassline or lead.

  5. 05

    Produce

    Pick a style card. The cloud builds a full arrangement around your transcription — and the drums are locked to your pattern, non-negotiable. Two variations back in about twenty seconds.

  6. 06

    Share the receipt

    One tap makes a vertical video with a before/after toggle: your mouth, then the track. Export MP3, WAV, MIDI or stems. Account sign-up only shows up here — never before.

04 / Two modes, one promise

Fidelity before flair

If we ever have to choose, we keep the groove and sacrifice polish. That's product principle number one, in writing.

Faithful mode

A deterministic render of your transcription. No generation, no surprises — your MIDI through twenty velocity-layered sampled kits, from acoustic and boom-bap to 808, Bollywood percussion and lo-fi.

  • Instant — plays ≤3 s after you stop recording
  • 100% on-device, works in airplane mode
  • Free forever, unlimited

Produce mode

A full arrangement — bass, harmony, texture, intro/loop/outro — generated around your beat, auto-mixed and loudness-normalized for streaming. Your transcribed drums are rendered deterministically inside it, so fidelity is structural, not statistical.

  • Eight styles at launch, two variations per render
  • Typically ~20 s for a 60-second track
  • Queues offline, renders when you reconnect

05 / Under the hood

Built like an instrument, not a slot machine

On-device transcription

Onset detection and five-class hit recognition (kick, snare, closed hat, open hat, perc) run locally in real time. Nine-plus classes — rimshot, clap, tom, crash, inward-K snare, lip roll — arrive in Phase 2.

Groove templates

Quantizing snaps hits to the grid but stores every micro-timing deviation, then re-applies it at render. Your swing survives the cleanup. Triplet grids are auto-detected.

Velocity that survives

Per-hit energy maps to the full 1–127 MIDI velocity range, normalized per class. The ghost notes stay ghosts; the accents stay loud.

60-second calibration

Optional. Record four examples of each sound and the classifier adapts to your mouth on the spot — no retraining, no upload. It's the accuracy ceiling-raiser, straight from the research.

Hum-to-melody

Monophonic pitch tracking with key inference and a scale-snap toggle turns a hummed pass into bassline or lead MIDI. Octave errors are handled for you.

Honest confidence

Noisy room? The app says “I probably missed a few — tap to fix” instead of failing silently, and stays useful down to street-level noise.

Real exports

MP3 on Free, WAV + standard MIDI on Pro, per-instrument stems on Studio. Artifacts you can drag into FL Studio or Ableton — the things text-to-music tools can't meaningfully give.

The A/B player

Toggle between original beatbox, faithful render and produced track with tempo-aligned switching. It's the demo that sells the app — and the share video is built on it.

Creation record

Export a bundle of your input recording, transcription and edit log. Because the track is built on your recorded human performance, your authorship position is real — and documented.

08 / Principles

The mic is sacred

Voice recordings are intimate. Here's the deal, in plain language — the same five principles the product is built on.

  1. Fidelity before flair. You must recognize your beat in the output. Styling sits on top of a transcription you can inspect and correct.
  2. Time-to-magic under 60 seconds. Every added screen before first playback is a bug, not a feature.
  3. Zero prerequisites, progressive depth. No account, calibration or music theory for track one. Depth reveals itself as you grow.
  4. The mic is sacred. On-device by default, explicit consent for any upload, one-tap deletion.
  5. Clean by construction. Licensed and commissioned training data only. We're the company that doesn't get sued.
  • On-device by default. Capture, transcription, editing and Faithful playback never touch a server.
  • Uploads are an action, not a default. Audio reaches the cloud only when you hit Produce or switch on sync.
  • Training is opt-in. Twice. Corrected patterns and raw audio have separate toggles. Neither is on out of the box.
  • Deletion means deletion. Within 30 days, including derived training copies.
  • Receipts on everything. C2PA content credentials and an inaudible watermark on all generated audio.
  • Never, at any price: voice cloning, “sounds like” artist prompts, impersonation. Permanent policy, not a settings page.
  • Compliant where you live. India DPDP, GDPR, CCPA; 13+ age gate with strictest defaults under 16.

11 / FAQ

Asked constantly

Do I need to know how to beatbox?

No. If you can say “boots and cats,” you can use BoomTap — and humming works too. Beatboxing is never treated as a skill requirement; the onboarding literally says “or just make sounds, we'll figure it out.”

Is my voice uploaded somewhere?

Not by default. Recording, transcription, editing and Faithful playback all happen on your phone. Audio goes to the cloud only when you explicitly hit Produce or enable sync, and contributing anything to model training is a separate opt-in — two of them, actually: one for corrected patterns, one for raw audio. Deletion requests are honored within 30 days, derived copies included.

Who owns the tracks I make?

You do, per the terms of service. And because every track is built on your recorded human performance — your groove, your melody — your authorship position is substantially stronger than with prompt-only generation. You can export a creation record (input recording, transcription, edit log) as evidence. Studio adds an explicit commercial-use grant.

How is this different from Suno or Udio?

Those tools treat uploaded audio as a vague vibe reference — melody and rhythm “referenced, not copied.” BoomTap's core is the opposite: a faithful, editable transcription of your performance that generation is then locked to. You can see what it heard, fix it, and export MIDI and stems. Different job entirely.

What was the AI trained on?

Licensed, commissioned or owned audio with written chain-of-title — no scraping, ever. Transcription models start from public research corpora (AVP, LVT, the Stowell beatbox corpus) plus a paid, consented recording program spanning 500+ participants and 25+ device models. Every generated file carries C2PA content credentials and an inaudible watermark.

What if it mishears me?

You'll see it immediately on the grid, and fixing a hit takes two taps. Zero-shot accuracy is around 80% per hit and climbs past 92% after an optional 60-second calibration where you teach the app your four core sounds. In noisy rooms the app flags low confidence instead of pretending — and every correction you make trains a better model, if and only if you've opted in.

Can I import an existing voice memo?

Yes — your own recordings, up to 60 seconds. Imports are fingerprint-screened before any cloud processing so copyrighted recordings can't be laundered through the pipeline.

When does Android arrive?

Phase 2, fast-follow after the iOS beta — Android 12+, tuned for mid-range hardware with a cloud-transcription fallback for low-end devices. The MVP targets iOS 16+ on iPhone 11 or newer.
